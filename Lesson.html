    What did I learn today?

    I learned about Cartier divisors today. If X is a Noetherian, integral, locally factorial, separated scheme, then its class group and the Cartier class group are the same. Since X being integral also implies its Cartier class group and Picard group are the same, we have a complete understanding. I also learned about the class group of a projective space, and any divisor of degree d will come as a multiple of a hyperplane.

    10/17/25: I learned how we define a Cartier divisor if we have an invertible sheaf. We can associate a subscheme of codimension 1 to an effective Cartier divisor, defined by the sheaf of ideals generated locally by \(f_i\)'s.
